CARD VALUES

Natural Cards - Any card with a number on it will have the same value as the number printed.

Face Cards - All face cards (J, Q, K) will have a value of 10.

Aces - The ace has a soft value of 1 or 11. The value of the card will be whichever value is larger that does not lead to the player busting (over 21).

GOAL OF THE GAME

  1. Get as close to having a total card value of 21 without going over.
  2. Beat the dealer.

WHAT CAN YOU DO?

  • Hit - In other words, you can ask for another card. At a table, this could be signaled by tapping twice on the table with two fingers.
  • Double Down - A riskier play than hitting, this requires you to double your bet on your hand. You only receive one extra card for that hand.
  • Split - Should you have two cards with the same value, you can double your bet and split your current hand into two playable hands.
  • Stay - Hold on to the card total you have. At a table, this can be signaled with a wave of your hand parallel to the table.
  • Surrender - You may forfeit your hand to receive half of you bet back.

FLOW OF THE GAME

  1. The dealer will deal out one face-up card out to each player twice in a clockwise rotation. On the first run, the dealer will deal themselves a face-up card. On the second, they will deal themselves a face-down card.
  2. If a player is showing a natural 21, or blackjack, that player will generally be awarded 1.5x their original bet. However, this can be stopped if the dealer also has blackjack.
  3. In the event the dealer's face-up card is an ace, they may ask the table if they would like to wager half of their current bet to buy insurance. If the dealer has a natural 21, or blackjack, all players who bought insurance will receive their bet back. Otherwise, each player that bought insurance loses their insurance bet and the hand continues.
  4. Following the clockwise rotation, each player will play their hand. Any player that has a total card value over 21 busts and is no longer a player in the hand.
  5. When the table has played, the dealer will reveal their face-down card. As long as the dealer is under 17 (verify with your table whether the dealer stops at soft 17+), the dealer must take a card.
  6. The dealer will pay all players that have beaten the dealer's hand their winnings at the end of the hand.
